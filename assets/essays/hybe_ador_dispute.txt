## Introduction

HYBE Corporation, originally founded as Big Hit Entertainment in 2005 by Si-hyuk Bang, is a prominent South Korean entertainment company known for shaping the global music industry through K-pop acts like BTS, TOMORROW X TOGETHER, and ENHYPEN. Rebranded in 2020, HYBE now operates under a multi-label system, including subsidiaries like Big Hit Music and Pledis Entertainment, allowing for diverse artist development. Known for its "HYBE 2.0" initiative, HYBE integrates technology and creativity to deepen fan engagement through platforms like Weverse, establishing it as a leader in artist-centered communities. However, recent internal conflicts bring ethics into focus, where ethical principles refer to the standards guiding HYBE's management of corporate and creative autonomy. This analysis explores how these conflicts impact HYBE's reputation and its responsibility to maintain ethical practices, considering its influence over artists, fans, and subsidiary labels in the K-pop industry.

In April 2024, an internal legal conflict between HYBE Corporation and its subsidiary ADOR generated major controversy within the K-pop industry. HYBE accused ADOR's CEO, Min Heejin, of attempting to gain control over ADOR and break away from HYBE, possibly taking the highly successful girl group NewJeans with her. Min Heejin, in response, claimed that HYBE copied NewJeans' unique concept for another group under a different subsidiary, ILLIT. This conflict escalated into legal complaints with allegations from both sides, including breach of trust, harassment, and embezzlement. A court issued an injunction temporarily preventing HYBE from firing Min Heejin, but the dispute remains ongoing, highlighting the tension between corporate control and creative independence in K-pop.

This essay investigates the following question: to what extent do internal conflicts and legal disputes between HYBE's headquarters and its subsidiary labels impact HYBE's brand image, ethical standing, and reputation within the K-pop industry? This question is explored through three business tools — Porter's Generic Strategies, Stakeholder Analysis, and Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) — to assess the strategic, stakeholder, and ethical dimensions of the conflict.

## Background and Financial Impact

The internal legal battle between HYBE and its subsidiary ADOR has significantly impacted HYBE's ethical standing and reputation within the K-pop industry, directly affecting its market position and investor confidence. On April 24, 2024, HYBE's stock price dropped by 5.75%, a decline of over 50% from its peak in November 2021. This sharp decrease reflects investor concerns regarding the potential long-term implications of internal conflicts. The dispute drew widespread media attention, both domestically and internationally, with coverage from outlets like the BBC, the New York Times, and Billboard, amplifying its negative impact on HYBE's global reputation.

Beyond financial repercussions, the HYBE-ADOR conflict exposed deeper issues within the K-pop industry, such as the balance between corporate control and creative autonomy, and raised concerns about artist welfare. The dispute, centered around NewJeans — a girl group with over 12 million followers on Instagram — highlights the potential impact of corporate conflicts on artists and their supporters, and underscores the challenges large conglomerates face in managing creative entities within corporate structures.

## Porter's Generic Strategy

![Porter's Generic Strategy Matrix](assets/images/essays/hybe_porters_matrix.png)

HYBE primarily pursues a differentiation strategy on a market scale, and a differentiation focus strategy within niche markets, distinguishing itself through global fan-engagement content and a multi-label system that allows for artistic diversity and creative autonomy. However, ongoing legal disputes with ADOR threaten this positioning. The legal battles risk eroding HYBE's cost advantage through legal fees and operational inefficiencies, while allegations of concept plagiarism tarnish its reputation for originality — undermining the artist-first, differentiated image that underpins its competitive position as a top entertainment label. In conclusion, HYBE's internal legal conflicts jeopardize its differentiation strategy by damaging its reputation for innovation, eroding trust with fans, and increasing operational costs. To maintain its competitive position, HYBE must address these conflicts transparently, restore its image of ethical leadership, and reinforce its commitment to original content.

## Stakeholder Analysis

Stakeholder analysis helps assess how HYBE's legal battles affect its ethical standing and reputation by examining the views of affected groups — shareholders, artists, employees, fans, media, industry peers, regulators, business partners, and investors.

Shareholders, as primary financial stakeholders, are deeply affected by HYBE's financial health, with the stock price dropping 5.75% and a total decline of over 50% since November 2021, raising concerns about the company's stability. Artists such as NewJeans face significant career implications due to the controversy, affecting their public image and future prospects. Employees within HYBE and its subsidiaries also feel the strain, facing uncertainty around job security as legal disputes disrupt operations and lower morale.

Externally, fans — particularly the large NewJeans fanbase — are conflicted about supporting HYBE amid the corporate dispute, as they may feel the company is placing corporate interests over artist welfare. Extensive media coverage amplifies these concerns and adds pressure on HYBE to respond transparently. Industry peers, regulators, business partners, and investors are all watching closely, with HYBE's handling of the situation shaping its industry standing and future collaboration and investment opportunities.

The diverse range of affected stakeholders amplifies the complexity of HYBE's situation, as financial concerns, artist welfare, and public trust converge. Effective management of these stakeholder relationships will be crucial for mitigating reputational damage and preserving HYBE's ethical standing.

## SWOT Analysis

A SWOT analysis helps assess how HYBE's legal battles impact its ethical standing by evaluating its strengths (brand loyalty), weaknesses (mistrust from legal issues), opportunities (rebuilding through transparency), and threats (loss of trust or partnerships).

HYBE's strengths include global brand recognition through acts like BTS, an innovative fan engagement platform in Weverse, and a multi-label system supporting diverse artist development. Its weaknesses lie in the ongoing internal conflicts and legal disputes that drain financial resources, the reputational damage from plagiarism allegations, and its heavy dependence on a small number of high-profile groups. Opportunities include expansion into international markets, diversification of revenue streams, and recruitment of new talent to reduce dependency on current top groups. Threats include competitive pressure from rival K-pop companies observing HYBE's handling of the dispute, increased regulatory scrutiny, and fan disengagement from perceived mistreatment of artists.

HYBE's strong global brand and innovative platform provide a solid foundation, but the current internal conflicts pose significant reputational challenges. By addressing these weaknesses and seizing new market opportunities, HYBE can strengthen its competitive edge while mitigating threats related to fan loyalty and regulatory scrutiny.

## Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) Analysis

![Carroll's CSR Pyramid](assets/images/essays/hybe_csr_pyramid.png)

Using Carroll's CSR pyramid, HYBE's economic responsibility to shareholders is under strain, given the financial losses and stock price decline from the dispute. Its legal responsibilities are technically being pursued through HYBE's lawsuits against ADOR, though the conflict raises questions about the effectiveness of HYBE's internal compliance mechanisms. Ethically, the dispute highlights tension between creative autonomy and corporate control — accusations of plagiarism and the controversial "Project 1945" document raise real questions about HYBE's ethical decision-making. At the philanthropic level, reputational and financial constraints from the dispute may limit HYBE's capacity to pursue meaningful social contributions.

The CSR analysis reveals that the internal legal battles have significantly impacted HYBE's performance across multiple CSR dimensions, with the economic and ethical dimensions particularly affected, weakening HYBE's position as a responsible corporate citizen.

## Conclusion

The internal legal battles within HYBE Corporation have significantly compromised its ethical standing and reputation in the K-pop industry. The conflict with its subsidiary ADOR led to a sharp decline in stock prices, raised concerns about the company's commitment to creative autonomy, and exposed potential ethical breaches — affecting both HYBE's financial performance and its public image in how it manages relationships with artists and subsidiaries.

Stakeholder analysis shows widespread concern among shareholders, artists, fans, and other industry players, further intensifying the negative impact on HYBE's reputation. Additionally, HYBE's CSR performance has been compromised, particularly in the economic and ethical dimensions, threatening its standing as a responsible corporate entity.

In conclusion, these internal conflicts have severely damaged HYBE's reputation. To recover, HYBE must enhance transparency, strengthen ethical practices, and recommit to supporting artistic integrity — steps that will be crucial for rebuilding trust and maintaining its leadership in the global entertainment industry.
